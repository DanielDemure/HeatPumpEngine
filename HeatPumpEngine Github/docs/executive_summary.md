# Executive Summary: Hexure Heat Pump Engine

## Project Overview
The Hexure Heat Pump Engine (HPE) is an innovative energy harvesting system that converts low-grade thermal energy into electricity through a novel CO2/acetone phase transition process. The system operates on a closed-cycle principle, utilizing the significant volumetric expansion of a CO2/acetone mixture at high pressure (70-120 bar) and moderate temperature (40Â°C) to drive a turbine-generator setup.

## Technical Innovation
The core innovation lies in leveraging the near-critical behavior of CO2-expanded liquids, specifically a CO2/acetone mixture, which exhibits up to 1000% volume expansion under the right thermodynamic conditions. This expansion is harnessed mechanically through a turbine, with the system designed to recover and reuse thermal energy through a secondary heat exchanger, enhancing overall efficiency.

## Market Opportunity
The HPE addresses critical global challenges in energy access and sustainability by:
- Enabling decentralized power generation from low-grade heat sources
- Reducing dependency on traditional grid infrastructure
- Providing a pathway for waste heat valorization in industrial processes
- Supporting off-grid and remote area electrification

## Development Status
The concept has been validated through theoretical analysis and literature review. Key findings include:
- Theoretical feasibility confirmed with positive net energy output in 95.8% of modeled scenarios
- Maximum net energy density of 90.0 kJ/kg achievable
- Minimum mass flow rate of 1.11 kg/s required for 100 kW output
